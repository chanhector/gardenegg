Fritz Hansen, a Danish furniture company, was founded in 1872. The business was already famous in the 1930s because of the work of Kaare Klint. After the Second World War it produced furniture by Hans J. Wegner and specialized in modern techniques of forming wood and combinations with steel tube construction. By collaborating with well-known Scandinavian designers such as B. Arne Jacobsen, Poul Kjaerholm, and Verner Panton, Hansen become an important furniture manufacturer in the 1960s and 1970s. Today the firm still produces prize-winning chairs and office furniture.
